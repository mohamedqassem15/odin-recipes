Pizza

Description

Indulge your taste buds in the delightful harmony of flavors with this homemade Margherita Pizza recipe. This classic Italian dish is a celebration of simplicity and quality ingredients, featuring a perfect balance of fresh tomato sauce, creamy mozzarella cheese, and fragrant basil leaves. The thin, crispy crust provides the ideal canvas for this culinary masterpiece, transporting you straight to the heart of Italy with every savory bite. Whether you're a seasoned chef or a kitchen novice, crafting this Margherita Pizza will not only satisfy your cravings but also elevate your culinary prowess.

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or torn
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can reach (usually around 475-500°F or 245-260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it's preheating.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a lightly floured surface to your desired thickness. If you're using a pizza peel and stone, transfer the rolled-out dough to the peel, ensuring it can slide easily onto the hot stone in the oven.
  3. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  4. Arrange the fresh mozzarella slices or torn pieces evenly on top of the sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated oven or onto the hot pizza stone. Bake for about 10-15 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  6. Once out of the oven, drizzle the pizza with extra-virgin olive oil and scatter fresh basil leaves on top. Sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese for an extra kick.
  7. Allow the pizza to cool for a few minutes before slicing, then serve and savor the authentic taste of a homemade Margherita Pizza. Buon Appetito!
